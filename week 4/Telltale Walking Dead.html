Telltale's Walking Dead

Desicion based Game

Summary


Telltale's The Walking Dead is a narrative-driven adventure game set in the post-apocalyptic world of The Walking Dead universe. It follows the story of Lee Everett, a convicted criminal who becomes a protector to a young girl named Clementine after a zombie outbreak. The game emphasizes choices, with players making decisions that affect the story and relationships with other characters. Over multiple seasons, the focus shifts to Clementine as she grows up and struggles to survive in a brutal, collapsing world. The game is known for its emotional storytelling, character-driven drama, and moral dilemmas.


History


Telltale's The Walking Dead launched in 2012, revolutionizing narrative-driven games with its episodic structure and focus on player choice. Developed by Telltale Games, it was based on Robert Kirkman’s comic series The Walking Dead. The first season received critical acclaim for its emotional depth and storytelling, particularly the bond between Lee Everett and Clementine. Telltale released four main seasons, with Clementine becoming the central character as the series progressed. The game’s success helped popularize episodic gaming and choice-driven narratives, influencing the broader gaming landscape, though Telltale itself faced financial struggles, eventually closing in 2018 before being revived.
